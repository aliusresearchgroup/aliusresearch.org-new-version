Membership Pathways

Membership Pathways

Two Complementary Pathways

ALIUS membership can be approached through two complementary pathways. One pathway emphasizes participation in the organizational and community life of the association, including voting participation and contribution to ongoing activities. The other pathway is research membership, oriented toward scholarly participation within the ALIUS research network.

These pathways are not meant to create rigid categories of belonging. They are practical frames that help people understand how they can enter the association and how their involvement may develop over time.

Choosing a Pathway

If you already know you want to contribute through organizational work, events, editorial projects, communications, or infrastructure, the participation-oriented pathway may be the most natural entry point. If your primary connection to ALIUS is through academic research on consciousness and non-ordinary states, research membership may be the better starting point.

Many members move across these pathways in practice. ALIUS encourages forms of participation that remain responsive to members' interests, collaborations, and changing circumstances.

Voting Membership

Voting Membership

Participation in ALIUS Governance

Voting membership supports the collaborative and member-driven functioning of ALIUS. Members participating through this pathway can contribute to discussions and votes about the association's development, including questions of organization, internal priorities, and the practical direction of shared activities.

This pathway is especially relevant for members who want to contribute not only to specific projects, but also to the long-term continuity and collective decision-making culture of the association.

Contribution and Participation

Voting participation can be combined with contributions across the domains of activity described below, including events, editorial work, communications, infrastructure, and community support. The aim is to make involvement meaningful and sustainable rather than to force members into fixed role templates.

Reduced membership contributions may be available depending on circumstances and level of contribution, in line with the ability-to-pay approach described in the membership contribution section.

Domains of Activity

Domains of Activity

ALIUS is sustained through member participation, but participation does not need to follow a rigid set of predefined roles. Members can define for themselves how they want to contribute, combine several kinds of activity, and adjust their involvement over time as projects and availability change.

Research and Scholarly Exchange

This domain includes scholarly dialogue, collaborative exploration of research questions, topic development, and participation in the intellectual life of ALIUS. It can also include strengthening links between researchers across disciplines and contributing to the conceptual direction of discussions around consciousness and non-ordinary states.

For some members this is the center of their involvement, while for others it is one component alongside work in events, editorial projects, or organizational stewardship.

Events and Program Development

This domain includes shaping symposia, workshops, and journal club activities through planning, scheduling, moderation, logistical support, and program curation. Work once described through specific titles, such as journal club coordination or event committee roles, can be understood here as examples of contribution formats rather than fixed slots to apply for.

Members may support a single event, a recurring activity, or a specific stage of an event pipeline, depending on capacity and interest.

Editorial and Publication Work

This domain includes interview coordination, editing, publication preparation, formatting, and the development of ALIUS editorial outputs. Bulletin editorial work remains an important example, but the emphasis is on the activity and collaboration involved rather than on maintaining a narrow role identity.

Members can contribute to editorial work in different ways, from short-term support to longer, more sustained involvement in publication workflows.

Media, Communications, and Outreach

This domain includes social media communication, media editing, documentation, and public-facing outreach that helps present ALIUS activities to wider audiences. Named functions such as social media or video editing are best understood as examples within this broader area of contribution.

Participation can range from occasional help with a campaign or recording to regular stewardship of communication channels.

Digital Infrastructure and Tools

This domain includes website maintenance, platform administration, email systems, technical workflows, and documentation. What was previously framed as a distinct infrastructure role can instead be shared across members and projects, making technical stewardship more resilient and collaborative.

Members with technical expertise can help reduce maintenance bottlenecks, improve reliability, and document systems so knowledge remains transferable within the association.

Community Support, Inclusion, Governance, and Finance

This domain brings together forms of stewardship that support the continuity of ALIUS, including member support, inclusion-oriented work, coordination, governance processes, and financial administration. Activities associated with coordinators, inclusion support, or treasurer responsibilities are examples of contributions within this shared stewardship space.

Some of this work is elected, some is delegated, and some emerges through ongoing collaboration. The common principle is that members can shape how they contribute while staying in dialogue with the coordinators and the membership team about current needs.

Self-Defined Contribution Pathways

You do not need to wait for a narrowly defined opening to contribute to ALIUS. If you have an idea for how you would like to support research exchange, events, editorial work, communications, infrastructure, or community life, you can propose your own pathway of participation and discuss how it could fit the association's priorities.

To discuss a domain of activity or a contribution plan, contact the membership team at memberships@aliusresearchgroup.org.

Research Membership

Research Membership

Research Membership in ALIUS

Research members form the core of ALIUS as an interdisciplinary research network concerned with the diversity of consciousness and non-ordinary states. Research membership supports scholarly exchange, visibility of members' work, and the development of collaborative opportunities across the association's activities.

ALIUS aims to function as a community-driven and career-building platform, supporting both collaboration and professional development while remaining open to a wide range of relevant disciplinary backgrounds.

Participation in Organizational Decisions

Research members participate in key organizational decisions concerning the functioning of ALIUS, including coordinator elections, amendments to rules, membership votes, and discussions around expenditures and association priorities. This reflects the collaborative and member-driven structure of the association.

These processes help maintain accountability while allowing the organization to evolve in response to member needs and ongoing projects.

Eligibility and Invitation Process

Prospective research members are generally expected to have an academic affiliation and to contribute to, or be informed by, the empirical study of consciousness and conscious states. New members typically require a referral from an existing member and are considered through a periodic membership vote.

The invitation process is intended to support coherence and continuity in the research community while still enabling interdisciplinary participation and growth.

Membership Contribution

Membership Contribution

Contribution Philosophy

ALIUS uses an ability-to-pay approach to annual membership contributions so that participation remains accessible while the association can sustain its activities. Members join from different financial situations, career stages, and institutional contexts, so the contribution guidance is meant to be supportive rather than rigid.

The emphasis is on fairness, solidarity, and continuity. Members are encouraged to choose a contribution level that reflects what is realistic for their current circumstances.

Suggested Contribution Ranges

A contribution in the range of 0 to €20 is appropriate for members with low income, uncertain academic positions, or periods in which financial flexibility is limited. This range also reflects the spirit of mutual support in cases where members contribute substantial time and labor to ALIUS activities.

A contribution around €50 is suggested for members with some disposable income and a level of stability that makes a moderate annual contribution feasible, including members who expect to remain in academic positions in the near future.

A contribution around €100 is suggested for members who can reimburse the cost through institutional funding or who are in more secure academic positions, including senior or tenured roles. This helps support accessibility across the broader membership community.

Flexibility and Context

The contribution level you choose can reflect changes in income, career stage, institutional reimbursement, and the forms of participation you are able to sustain within ALIUS. Because participation is framed through domains of activity rather than fixed role applications, contribution and involvement can evolve together in a practical and non-hierarchical way.

The purpose of the contribution guidance is to support long-term participation and collective sustainability, not to impose a single financial expectation on a diverse membership base.

Questions About Contributions

If you are unsure which contribution level makes sense for your circumstances, or if you would like to discuss membership renewal in relation to your participation pathway, please contact the membership team at memberships@aliusresearchgroup.org. We are happy to help you find an appropriate approach.

If you want to discuss eligibility, research membership, contribution guidance, or the best way to shape your participation in ALIUS, contact the membership team directly by email.
